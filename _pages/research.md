My research moves between the bedside and the bench, with one guiding question:
**how does fibrosis begin, progress, and become irreversible — and can we change
that trajectory for patients?**

## Lung fibrosis and fibroblast biology

Fibroblasts are central effectors of pulmonary fibrosis. My laboratory work focuses
on the signals that push these cells toward a pathological, fibrosis-driving state.

- **Angiopoietin-like 4 (ANGPTL4)** as a critical regulator of fibroblasts during
  pulmonary fibrosis development.
- **Insulin-like growth factor 1 (IGF-1)** produced by lung fibroblasts from patients
  with IPF, and its contribution to fibrosis.

## Clinical and translational research

- Prognosis of **fibrotic ILD**, including the long-term prognostic impact of the
  **Clinical Frailty Scale** on acute exacerbation.
- **Development of a novel in vitro diagnostic for idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis**
  (Early-Career Researcher Grant, Japan Research Foundation for Clinical Pharmacology).

## Vision — toward a cross-organ understanding of fibrosis

Fibrosis is not confined to the lung. The same fundamental program — fibroblast
activation, tissue stiffening, and loss of function — recurs across many organs.
My longer-term aim is to study **fibrosis as a cross-organ phenomenon**, connecting
mechanisms learned in the lung to fibrotic disease elsewhere in the body.
